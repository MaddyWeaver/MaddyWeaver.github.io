ABOUT ME

Opportunity Is For the Resourceful

My name is Madeleine Weaver, and I was born near Boston, Massachusetts. When I was growing up my mother taught me that an understanding of creation and repair, innovation as resourcefulness, is a way to have experiences you could not otherwise. She found a neglected boat in the classifieds and together we worked to restore it, patching the fiberglass and painting the hull with barnacle repellant paint. We would call our family friend 'Uncle Arthur' to see if he had found any more motor parts at the take-it-or-leave-it section of our town transfer center that he could help us knit together. When we finally got the boat sea-worthy we spent our summer weekends together exploring a cove in Rhode Island, and knowing that our resourcefulness enabled these adventures is what inspires me to continue learning and creating.

My primary interests are robotics and plasma research. I am interested in the intersection of these two fields in application to space exploration and research, and the potential for plasma fusion research to lead us to a new form of clean energy production. With each new experience, I try to gain skills and experience in fabrication because I feel most fulfilled when I have built a physical system from start to finish. I love making things that allow us to see or experience things we would not otherwise have the oportunity to. I love projects that push the limits of what is possible, practical, or probable.
